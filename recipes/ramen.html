Description

Ramen is a Japanese noodle soup dish that originated in China but evolved into a distinctly Japanese comfort food.

Ramen varies widely by region in Japan (e.g., Sapporo's miso ramen, Hakata's tonkotsu) and can range from humble street stalls to gourmet creations. Instant ramen (like Top Ramen or Cup Noodles) is a globally popular convenience version.

Ingredients

  • Noodles — Wheat-based, alkaline (kansui) noodles that are springy and chewy, ranging from thin and straight to thick and wavy depending on the style.
  • Broth — A deeply flavored soup base, often slow-simmered for hours
  • Chashu (braised pork belly)
  • Ajitama (soft-boiled marinated egg)

Steps

  1. Knead the dough into noodles
  2. Boil some salted water
  3. Add the noodles in, then cook with toppings
  4. Eat and enjoy
